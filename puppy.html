Woof Love

When the time comes to finally bring your new puppy home for the first time, you can pretty much count on three things: unbridled joy, cleaning up your puppy’s accidents, and a major lifestyle adjustment.Lets see some tips to make your life much easier!

1.Finding a vet

  • Set up a vaccination plan with your vet.
  • Discuss safe options for controlling parasites, both external and internal.
  • Learn which signs of illness to watch for during your puppy’s first few months.
  • Ask about when you should spay or neuter your dog.

2.Watch For Early Signs of Illness

If you observe any of the following symptoms in your puppy, it’s time to contact the vet.
  • Lack of appetite
  • Poor weight gain
  • Vomiting
  • Swollen of painful abdomen
  • Lethargy (tiredness)
  • Diarrhea
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Wheezing or coughing

3.Establish a Bathroom Routine

Housetraining quickly becomes a high priority on most puppy owners’ list of must-learn tricks.
Make sure to give lots of positive reinforcement whenever your puppy manages to potty outside and, almost equally important, refrain from punishing her when she has accidents indoors.
Here’s a list of the most common times to take your puppy out to potty.
  • When you wake up.
  • Right before bedtime.
  • Vomiting
  • Immediately after your puppy eats or drinks a lot of water.
  • When your puppy wakes up from a nap.
  • During and after physical activity.

4.Teaching Obidence

  • By teaching your puppy good manners, you’ll set your puppy up for a life of positive social interaction.
  • In addition, obedience training will help forge a stronger bond between you and your puppy.
  • Teaching your pup to obey commands such as sit, stay, down, and come will not only impress your friends, but these commands will help keep your dog safe and under control in any potentially hazardous situations
